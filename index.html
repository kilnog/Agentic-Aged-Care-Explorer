What happens when we stop
asking humans to do everything?

A hypothetical explorer for agentic thinking in aged care — and every industry beyond it.

Purpose

This is a thinking tool,
not a product pitch.

The Aged Care MDT Explorer was built to answer a simple question: if AI agents could handle the cognitive and administrative weight of a role, what would humans be freed to do instead?

It is entirely hypothetical. No real residents, no real data, no implementation spec. Every workflow, confidence threshold, and agent model in this tool is illustrative — a provocation, not a prescription.

The goal is to make the abstract concrete. To take a vague idea like "AI in healthcare" and walk it through a real Tuesday morning in an aged care facility, so that the conversation shifts from fear to curiosity.

🔬
Invoke deep thinking
Not surface-level automation. Real workflow analysis. Where does cognition happen? Where is judgement required? Where is physical presence irreplaceable?
🧭
Expand limiting beliefs
Most resistance to AI comes from imagining it doing everything or nothing. This tool models the middle — where agents and humans complement each other.
🌐
Translate to any industry
The dual-layer model — agent handles cognitive load, human handles physical and legal responsibility — applies to construction, law, education, retail, logistics, and beyond.
The core idea

Agents don't replace humans.
They change what humans do.

01
The cognitive tax is real
A registered nurse spends an estimated 35–40% of their shift on documentation, scheduling, and information retrieval — tasks that consume mental energy but don't require their clinical expertise. An agent can absorb most of that.
02
The human gate is sacred
Every agent in this framework has a hard stop — a point where a physical human must act, sign, decide, or be present. Not because the agent can't process the information, but because accountability, touch, and judgement belong to people.
03
The shift is already happening
Clinicians already use digital documentation, alert systems, and scheduling software. Agentic AI is the next layer — not a rupture, but an evolution of tools that have been accumulating for decades.

GP / In-House GP Agent Handoff Model
The agentic model creates an in-house 'aged care GP skill' agent that handles low-level reasoning, PRN queries, and routine GP communication — reducing the dependency on physical GP availability.
IN-HOUSE GP AGENT — scope
→ Fields PRN medication queries (within safe parameters)
→ Flags drug interactions and contraindications
→ Prepares structured updates for physical GP visits
→ Handles routine GP communication and callbacks
→ Supports agency staff with GP contact protocols
→ Maintains GP visit list with auto-updated resident summaries
IN-HOUSE GP AGENT — never does
→ Diagnose or prescribe
→ Approve changes beyond pre-set safe parameters
→ Replace the physical GP relationship
→ Make decisions on novel or complex clinical scenarios
Benefits for agency staff
Agency staff often lack knowledge of facility-specific GP contacts and escalation protocols. The in-house agent provides: correct GP contact, when to escalate, SLA and regulatory obligations, and audit trail — even for staff who have never worked at the facility before.

HITL gate — always applies
Human reviews brief before any call
Agent cannot make the phone call or send a notification directly to family without human review. The RN or Admin reviews the draft brief, modifies if needed, and confirms it's appropriate and complete. This gate is mandatory — family contact always requires a human decision.
Human RN / Admin — HITL
Human makes the call
RN or Admin phones the family
The human makes the actual phone call. The agent brief is on-screen as a prompt. The call is conducted by a person — the agent does not use synthetic voice or auto-dial. After the call, the human logs the outcome (call made, voicemail, callback requested, family concern raised).
Human RN / Admin
Post-call — agent
Agent documents communication record
Agent pre-fills communication log from the human's call notes. Documents: who was contacted, time, what was communicated, any family concerns or decisions, follow-up actions required. Flags if family requested a callback or a meeting.
Documentation Agent
Follow-up — agent
Agent schedules follow-up actions
If family requested a care meeting, callback, or GP update — agent creates the follow-up task and assigns it to the appropriate role. Ensures nothing falls through the shift-change gap. Tracks outstanding family communication items as a live queue.

AFTER-HOURS PROTOCOL
Agent determines urgency level. Low urgency: queues for next business day. Medium: flags for on-call staff to review. High urgency (hospitalisation, death, serious incident): immediate HITL escalation — on-call staff must be contacted regardless of time. Agent cannot determine if a situation warrants an after-hours call — that decision is always human.

Pre-Admission Agentic Workflow
Multi-disciplinary agentic workflow from initial referral through clinical assessment, financial review, and admission decision. Includes a sample AI-generated pre-admission report.
Pre-admission agentic process
Agent roles in pre-admission
DOCUMENT ANALYSIS AGENT
Reads ACAT, nursing summary, GP healthcare summary. Extracts clinical needs, medications, behavioural flags, mobility status. Outputs structured clinical summary in minutes, not 30+ minutes of manual reading.
SUITABILITY SCORING AGENT
Scores the referral across: clinical complexity vs facility capability, behavioural needs, AN-ACC classification estimate, staff skill match. Produces suitability rating with reasoning.
FINANCIAL ASSESSMENT AGENT
Checks room availability, estimated AN-ACC funding, basic accommodation suitability. Flags financial team for means assessment. Does not make financial decisions.
HUMAN DECISION (CCM + RN)
Review agent one-pager. Clinical validation check. Yes/no decision takes ~5 min vs 30+ min without agent. Human judgement still essential — agent output is advisory only.
TIME SAVING ESTIMATE
Pre-assessment review: 30 min → ~5 min (agent summary)
Full admission assessment: 4–8 hrs (physical still required)
Admin coordination: ~2 hrs → ~30 min (agent drafts comms)

The full picture
No single carer, nurse, or GP knows all of this about Joe at once. On any given Tuesday morning the RN arriving for their shift might not know he refused breakfast three days running, that David prefers morning calls, that the advance care plan is overdue for review, or that his mood scores have trended down for six weeks. The agentic system doesn't replace the human who sits with him, holds his hand, and knows his voice. It makes sure that human arrives informed — so the time they do have together is spent on what only they can give him.
